Traveling Surgery Team Partnership Guidelines

Key Responsibilities for Local Partners

Requesting a Veterinary Team: Key Responsibilities

It is a core requirement that international animal groups and/or individuals secure local resources when requesting a veterinary team. Support from the local government and/or community is highly valued. These factors make a project more desirable, help the traveling surgery team develop new projects more efficiently, and are often crucial in determining project feasibility.

Legality & Licenses

International animal groups and/or individuals are responsible for investigating their government's policy regarding foreign veterinarians volunteering services within the country. They are also required to secure any necessary licenses and/or permits required by law to import veterinary supplies.

Safety

Safety is the number one priority for our veterinary teams. We do not plan projects in areas that are at war or have a high threat of terrorist activity. We consult the US State Department travel advisory website for all project locations and, as a general rule, do not plan projects in areas with a travel warning.

Local Veterinary Community

Local partners need to establish whether a local veterinarian and/or individual practicing veterinary medicine is available in the area. Local veterinarians are expected to be supportive of a traveling surgery team program and are required to coordinate and partake in our activities. The traveling surgery team must complement and enhance available veterinary resources, not compete with them.

Operating Budget

Local partners need to establish their own operating budget for a traveling surgery team clinic as they are responsible for providing some items locally. Local partners should actively seek donations from within their community. Monetary donations received during the clinic will be given to the local coordinating charity.

Language

The majority of traveling surgery team volunteers only speak English. Local partners are expected to ensure at least one local bilingual volunteer is present during a **traveling surgery team** clinic.

Ongoing Requirements & Logistics

Controlled Drugs

Local partners are required to investigate and inform the traveling surgery team whether certain controlled drugs, such as ketamine and valium, can be purchased locally and for what amount. Purchasing these locally is ideal.

Accessibility

Local partners need to confirm that the requested location is easily accessible for a veterinary team. They are responsible for providing information on the time and logistical requirements necessary to arrive at the final destination.

Time of Year

Weather plays a significant role. We will avoid the rainy season and **will not schedule** a project around the time when local or national elections are being held, or during major celebrations.

Local Activities

Local partners are responsible for assisting in organizing activities for the veterinary team during their leisure time (roughly 2-3 days). Local partners are required to provide a list of activities ahead of time.

Required Local Provisions (Page 3)

A few things must be provided locally by international animal groups to ensure the successful execution of a traveling surgery team clinic:

Building/Room

  • Ideal space is at least 900 sq ft or adjacent rooms providing that much space (for small animal projects).
  • Access to electricity and running water and bathroom facilities is a necessity. Alternatives must be identified if not available.

Local Transportation

Local partners must coordinate local transportation prior to the veterinary team's arrival for:

  • Airport Transfers (to and from).
  • Clinic Work Days (to and from the clinic location, if not walkable).

A van/microbus for 12-18 people is ideal. Donations of transportation should be sought.

Lunches and Drinks

During clinic work days (typically 3 days), local partners are responsible for providing lunch for 18-25 people and cold drinks.

Safety Requirement: Local partners **must be certain** the meals are coming from a sanitary source.

Accommodations

Local partners **need to ensure** there is a place for a team of 12-18 people to stay. Assistance in finding suitable, discounted lodging (a large vacation rental is preferred) is expected.

Supplies, Volunteers, and Promotion (Page 4)

Required Local Supplies

  • 5-7 tables for surgery/prep
  • 1L of hydrogen peroxide
  • 1-2L of isopropyl alcohol
  • Paper towels
  • Garbage bags
  • General cleaning spray (Windex)
  • 2 dish tubs (for cleaning instruments)
  • Fans (if applicable)
  • Tarps or old towels and sheets (recovery)
  • Bricks for elevating tables (16-20 bricks)
  • Dog kennels and/or animal cages (availability confirmation needed)

Local Volunteers

Local partners **need to secure** local volunteers for clinic work days for the following activities:

  • Patient Check-In
  • Transport animals to and from the clinic
  • Translators
  • Publicize/promote the veterinary services

Advertising

Local partners **are responsible for advertising and promoting** the veterinary services **before** the veterinary team arrives. This is essential to ensure a good turnout. Suggested media outlets include newspaper, radio, flyers, and door-to-door.

Preparing for a Veterinary Team: Education & Raising Awareness (Page 5)

This responsibility **primarily rests** with the local coordinating group. Local partners should utilize community members who use the free services as advocates.

Local partners **should appeal** to community members by relating animal health and wellbeing issues to their own lives, pointing out the benefits for them and the overall improvement of their community. (See Appendix 3 for talking points).

Local partners **should run** an advertising campaign for 2–4 weeks prior to the visit. The key to success is maintaining the issue of animal health on the minds of the community.

Harnessing & Maintaining Local Support

Local partners **need to recognize** the community members and/or businesses that volunteer or donate resources. Showing appreciation and making them feel like a part of the success is essential for strengthening their ongoing support.

Starter Checklist for Local Partners (Pages 6-7)

Project & Planning

  • Aid Required: Small animal, Equine, Livestock, Ruminants, Wildlife (check all that apply).
  • Accommodations: Is decent, secure lodging available for 12-18 people?
  • Budget: Local partners **are responsible for calculating** necessary funds and **need to identify** donated items.
  • Legal Paperwork: Local partners **are required to confirm and file** any necessary legal paperwork.
  • Clinic Location Confirmation: Needs to confirm space (>900 sq ft), Electricity, Water, Working toilet, and Security.

Logistics & Supplies

  • Transportation: Must be coordinated for Airport (arrival/departure) and Clinic days.
  • Lunch and Drinks: Local partners **are responsible for ensuring** food/drinks/extra tables/cooler for 3 clinic days.
  • Check In Sheets: Need to ensure enough paper and access to a printer to make about 150 copies.
  • Local Supplies to Provide: 5-7 tables, hydrogen peroxide, isopropyl alcohol, paper towels, garbage bags, cleaning spray, dish tubs, fans, tarps/sheets, and dog kennels (if available).
  • Volunteers to Secure: Patient check in, Transport, Translator(s), and Organizing activities.
  • Advertising: Local partners **are responsible for developing** an effective strategy to inform everyone in town.

Clinic Work Stations & Procedures (Pages 8-9)

Designate a "Clinic Manager"

The designated Clinic Manager **is accountable for** the following core responsibilities:

  • Act as the main contact for the traveling surgery team Field Service Veterinarian and the local community.
  • Direct and supervise all local volunteers.
  • Oversee and manage all animals entering and leaving the clinic.
  • Communicate with Patient Check In and Recovery regarding patient flow and release.
  • Communicate with pet owners about the process and aftercare.
  • Organize and ensure local support (lunch, transportation, supplies) is present.

Patient Check In (Reception)

This station **is required to be located** at the clinic entrance (table, chairs, and local volunteers needed).

  • Greet and **register** each pet owner.
  • **Fill out 2 separate forms**: general roster (stays at reception) and surgery form (accompanies the animal).
  • **Ask about** previous health concerns.
  • **Inform and provide** written and spoken take-home instructions.

Veterinary Service Stations

Stations include Pre-Med, Surgery, Cleaning, Recovery, and Medical Consultations. Local veterinarians and students **are encouraged to participate** in areas like preparation, consultations, cleaning, and recovery.

Opportunities for local vets/vet students to practice surgery **will be determined** by the Field Service Veterinarian and are not standard practice during first participation.

After a Veterinary Team Visit (Page 10)

Aftercare

Local partners **need to ensure** that pet owners are given both **written and spoken instructions**. Local partners **should visit** or **call pet owners** to check on animal recovery. The veterinary team is typically available for 1–2 days after the clinic for follow-up.

Evaluation & Feedback

Both local partners and the **traveling surgery team** **should evaluate** all project aspects. Local partners **are expected to share** the clinic results, including the number of animals and types of cases seen, as well as photos.

Planning Next Visit

Planning is done with a designated staff member and **should incorporate** evaluation feedback. Follow-up visits **may be canceled** if the **traveling surgery team** faces insurmountable challenges (logistics, local authorities, etc.).

After Surgery Instructions (For Pet Owners)

Your pet has just undergone an operation with anesthesia. It is critical to take proper care of your pet for a minimum of 1 week following surgery.

Section Instructions
WATER & FOOD Anesthesia can cause an upset stomach. **You must restrict** food for the first 3 hours. **You must gradually increase** their food and water each day.
BATHE/GET WET **Do not** let your pet get wet or bathe them for a minimum of 7 days after surgery.
PHYSICAL ACTIVITY **You must restrict** physical activity for 7 days. Dogs **must be kept** on a leash. Cats **must be put** in a small, confined area.
BE CAREFUL OF THE INCISION **You must examine** the incision twice a day. If excessive bleeding/inflammation occurs, **you must contact a veterinarian**. **Do not** let your pet lick the incision. The dissolvable suture **does not need to be removed**.
MEDICINE **Do not give** Aspirin or Tylenol unless specifically prescribed. Take-home pain medication **can be obtained** from the veterinary team.
